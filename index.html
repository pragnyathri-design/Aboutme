T Cell Every T-cell has its own unique receptor — about 10¹⁵ possible versions, more than grains of sand on Earth.
Natural Killer Cell NK cells kill on sight — no antigen check needed. They spot cells that stopped displaying "self" and punch a hole in them within minutes.
Macrophage The name means "big eater" in Greek — a single macrophage can engulf and digest over 100 bacteria before it dies.
B Cell B cells are the antibody factories — a single activated B cell can pump out 2,000 antibodies per second.
Cancer Cell HeLa cells, taken from Henrietta Lacks in 1951, are still dividing in labs today — the first "immortal" human cell line.
Dendritic Cell Dendritic cells are the immune system's messengers — they take samples from your tissues and show them to T-cells to decide what to attack.
